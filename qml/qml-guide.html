Quick Meeting Links - Complete Guide

Authentication

Quick Meeting Links requires authentication with your Google account to:

  • Access your Google Calendar
  • Create and manage calendar events
  • Generate Google Meet links

The extension uses Google's secure OAuth 2.0 protocol for authentication, ensuring your data remains protected.

Operating Modes

1. With Entry Mode

Creates both a calendar event and a Meet link. Ideal for scheduled meetings where you want to:

  • Set specific date and time
  • Have the meeting appear in your calendar
  • Send calendar invitations to participants

2. No Entry Mode

Generates only a Meet link without creating a calendar event. Perfect for:

  • Quick, impromptu meetings
  • When you don't need calendar tracking
  • Instant video calls

3. Duo Mode

Creates two synchronized calendar entries:

  • Main calendar: Contains the actual meeting with the Meet link
  • Secondary calendar: Stores meeting details and link for easy reference

Perfect for scenarios with shared calendars where meeting hosts might differ from event creators. For example:

  • Create an event in a shared calendar for visibility
  • Store the Meet link separately so a moderator can start the meeting
  • Solve the common issue where non-organizers can't admit participants to meetings

This mode ensures smooth meeting management while maintaining proper calendar organization.

Using the Extension

Basic Setup

  1. Select your preferred calendar from the dropdown menu
  2. Choose the meeting start date and time
  3. Set the meeting end time
  4. Enter a meeting title
  5. Click "Generate Link" to create your meeting

Note: The extension will automatically copy to clipboard the generated link.

Meeting History

The "Show Meeting History" feature allows you to:

  • View all previously generated meeting links
  • Export meeting history to CSV format for record-keeping
  • Clear history when needed

Logging Out

The Logout function revokes the extension's access to your Google account

Duo Mode Settings

In Duo Mode, meetings are synchronized between two calendars. This is especially useful when working with shared calendars.

Common Use Case:

  • You create meetings in a shared calendar (like "Hidden Meetings")
  • The Meet link is stored in your Hidden Meetings calendar
  • A copy appears in your team's calendar for visibility
  • You maintain host privileges through the Hidden Meetings calendar

Department Use Case:

  • Executive assistants storing meetings in their Hidden Meetings calendar
  • Meeting details are displayed in the department calendar
  • Assistant maintains host access through Hidden Meetings
  • Perfect for managing multiple calendars while maintaining meeting control

Important:

  • Additional participants need to be invited manually after event creation
  • Perfect for maintaining host access while sharing with one team/department

Settings Management

  • "Save" - Preserves your custom settings
  • "Restore to Defaults" - Resets all settings to their original values

Privacy & Data Security

Important: Quick Meeting Links operates with complete privacy:

  • All data is stored locally on your device
  • No data is sent to external servers
  • Your calendar and meeting information remains private
  • The extension only accesses the permissions you grant it
